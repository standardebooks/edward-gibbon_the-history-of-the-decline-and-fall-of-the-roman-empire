Endnote references were expanded to include full author names in their first occurrence, full publication names (if able to be determined) in their first occurrence, and elimination of abbreviations in all remaining author and publication names where possible. A few author names were corrected to their modern spelling (e.g. Leclerc, Jerome), and a few Chinese names were converted to Wade-Giles spelling.

The transcription was missing all italics, diacritics, and Greek quotations; these have all been restored. None of the non-English quotations (Latin, French, German, etc.) were identified or marked, these have all been tagged and italicized. Thousands of transcription typos both in the text and primarily the endnotes were corrected via a side-by-side review of the scans/text, and a second proofreading pass after the corrections.

Latin words/abbreviations beginning with ad (e.g. ad loc.) and in (e.g. in loc.) have been tagged and italicized, in keeping with SE's policy of tagging Latin abbreviations that begin with English words, e.g. ad hominem, in extremis, etc., to prevent confusion.

Gibbon often introduces subjects (usually people groups) using italics. Per a discussion with the Editor-in-Chief on the list, this italicization (as emphasis) has been kept. Since Gibbon typically only italicizes the first occurrence, this seemed the best course to take.

At attempt was made to standardize punctuation on references. Those of type “Author/Work X 1” or “Author, Work X 1” have only a comma between Author and Work in the latter case. Those of “Author/Work, l./tom./etc. X [c. Y][, p. Z]”, “Author, Work, l./tom./etc. X [c. Y][, p. Z]” have comma between author and work, between author/work and volume, and before page number(s).

Because Gibbon jumps around in the chronology of the history, shoulder notes from the Smith edition below were added to the text, containing the date(s) the events under discussion take place. The notes contain only the date(s), and usually only when the year changes.

The PG transcription included chapter titles. These do not exist in any of the scans/printed editions, so were removed.
